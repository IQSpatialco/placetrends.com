Spatial Statistics

Spatial Statistics is a branch of statistics that deals with the analysis of spatial data, focusing on the statistical properties and relationships of geographically referenced observations. It provides methods for describing, analyzing, and modeling spatial patterns, spatial dependence, and spatial heterogeneity in data.

This field recognizes that spatial data often violates the independence assumption of classical statistics due to spatial autocorrelation and spatial effects. Spatial statistics provides specialized techniques and tests to properly analyze spatial data, account for spatial relationships, and draw valid statistical inferences from geographic information.

Practical Applications

  • Environmental monitoring and pollution pattern analysis
  • Epidemiological studies and disease cluster detection
  • Agricultural research and crop yield analysis
  • Economic geography and regional development studies
  • Ecology and species distribution modeling

Related Terms
